About

The studio,
briefly

Founded in Lisbon, 2017. Seven people across two cities. Independent. Booking Q3.

Slow studio,
careful work.

Object was founded in Lisbon in 2017 by two former Pentagram associates who, frankly, missed the print room. Nine years on, we are seven people working out of a converted printers' cooperative in Alfama and a small office above a bagel shop in the East Village. We have remained, and intend to remain, small.

Our work spans brand, identity, editorial, type design, exhibition, and book — disciplines that share a common ancestor in letters arranged on a page. We tend to take on six to eight engagements a year, working closely with one or two clients at a time. Most projects last between four and eighteen months.

"The aim has never been to grow. The aim has been to make work that lasts longer than the deadline."

We've been recognized in the usual places — D&AD, ADC, TDC, AIGA — but the recognition we care about is the kind that arrives in the form of a book, an exhibition catalog, or a magazine that someone in another city thumbs through fifteen years from now and wonders who made this.

You can find a more complete version of the studio's history, its principles, and its colophon in the printed studio book, available on request.

What we
practice.

i.
Brand & identity

Wordmarks, monograms, identity systems, and the small typographic decisions that make the difference between a brand that lasts and one that doesn't. Usually the start of a long relationship.

→
ii.
Editorial design

Magazines, newspapers, journals, annual reports, monographs. Designing for reading more than for looking. Long projects, careful proofs.

→
iii.
Type design

Custom typefaces drawn for specific institutions and publications. We've drawn faces for The Met, Le Monde, and a small Portuguese publishing house. Slow, in-house, and licensed properly.

→
iv.
Book design

Trade and museum-published books. Hardcover, softcover, multi-volume sets, and the occasional accordion fold. Working with letterpress where the budget allows.

→
v.
Exhibition & environment

Wayfinding, signage, vinyl, and the typography of public space. We work alongside architects but make our own object decisions.

→
vi.
Digital systems

Editorial websites, brand sites, and design systems for institutions whose work happens online. Hand-coded with our developer Lúcia in Lisbon.
