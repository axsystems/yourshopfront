The dough

Type 00 flour from Naples. Cold-fermented 48 hours. Hand-stretched, never rolled. We make about 200 dough balls a day.

The oven

An Acunto Mario wood-fired oven, imported from Naples in 1989. 800°F. We burn cherry & oak. Pies cook in 90 seconds.

The tomato

San Marzano DOP, packed in San Marzano. We don't blend, we don't sauce. Crushed by hand the morning of.

↗ Three generations

A family business,
since the year Eisenhower
was reelected.

Angelo Esposito Sr. opened the doors of Angelo's in October of 1956, in a storefront on Sixth Avenue that had been a barber shop the week before. He brought one recipe from Naples — his grandmother Lina's — and one wood-fired oven, which he assembled in the back of the shop with bricks salvaged from a demolition on Fifth.

For sixty-eight years, the recipe has not changed. The oven has been rebuilt twice. The original counter, the chairs, the tomato can on the shelf — all of it is still here. We are the third generation.

"My grandfather said the menu was finished in 1956. My father added one pie. I've added one. That's the rate of change my family is comfortable with."

— Angelo Esposito III, the current pizzaiolo, 2026

↗ How to visit

Pickup only.
Walk-ups welcome.

We've never delivered, we never will. Pickup only. Tuesday through Sunday, eleven to eleven. Closed Mondays — that's when Lina rests and we clean the oven.

Order ahead by phone or on the website. Pies are ready in roughly twenty minutes. If we run out of dough, we close early. That's the deal.
